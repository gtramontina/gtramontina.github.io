No Getters nor Setters

Note: This post is part of the series Lessons (re)learned.

A lot has been said about the perils of using getters and setters—also known as accessors and mutators—in object oriented software. To cite a couple: Allen Holub, back in 2003, published "Why getter and setter methods are evil"; Yegor Bugayenko, a decate later, wrote "Getters/Setters. Evil. Period.".
